1 Introduction

1.1 Module Goal

This module provides guidance for the development and management of essential documentation before, during, and at the close of a randomized clinical trial (RCT).

1.2 General Considerations

Documentation provides validation that research is conducted maintaining the highest ethical and clinical standards as established by the World Health Organization and the International Conference on Harmonization (ICH) guidelines for Clinical Good Practice (GCP)

Documentation requirements will vary according to several factors, such as intervention type (e.g., drug, device, behavior) and sponsor (e.g., NIH, drug company). Thus, you should verify that your documentation plan complies with the specific nature of your RCT. For example, IND Safety Reports only pertain to those studies conducted under an FDA IND. Documentation requirements in clinical research are specified in regulations such as DHHS OHRP. See resource section below for link to FDA website.

The ICH GCP, section 8, “Essential Documents for the Conduct of a Clinical Trial” provides a full listing of documents that are generally deemed essential. These documents demonstrate that the trial conduct complies with the standards of GCP and all regulatory requirements. This module focuses on describing the key Essential Documents required for a clinical RCT, including:

  • Study Protocol
  • Manual of Operations
  • Statistical Analytic Protocol
  • Data Safety Monitoring Board Charter and Regular Reports (refer to other chapter for details)
  • Event Tracking
